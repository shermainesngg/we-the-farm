## Design Context

### Users
Community members in Singapore — volunteers, neighbours, families, students — discovering or joining a rooftop farm. Browsed casually on phones and desktops, often through Instagram links.

### Brand Personality
Rooted, communal, sun-warmed. A passion project, not a corporate venture. Feels like a hand-printed farmers' market poster on thick recycled stock — scrappy and full of personality.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial farm-poster inspired by Soul & Soil Composting and Flor & Fjære. Bold, poster-like typography with dramatic size contrast. Organic decorative shapes (squiggles, blobs, asterisks) scattered throughout. Marquee text strips for energy. Olive-toned palette (not pure forest green). Asymmetric editorial layouts with numbered process steps and alternating image/text blocks. Light theme (daytime outdoor community context).

### Typography
- **Headings**: Young Serif — warm display serif with ink traps and handcrafted character, weight 400
- **Body**: Figtree — friendly geometric sans with rounded terminals, variable 300–700

### Color Palette
- Forest/Olive: #3B4A2B (primary olive green, hero overlays, dark sections)
- Forest Light: #5C6D3F (medium olive, hover states)
- Leaf/Moss: #7A8F5D (soft green accents)
- Sage: #A4B88C (light green accents)
- Cream: #FAF3E3 (primary background)
- Parchment: #F0E6CE (secondary surface)
- Terracotta: #D05E3A (CTA accent, decorative shapes, step numbers)
- Earth: #2C1D10 (very dark brown, headings, dark sections)
- Amber: #D4A130 (golden accent, hero "Sky" text)
- Honey: #EBC85B (light gold labels)
- Soil: #3A2718 (body text)

### Design Principles
1. **Poster-bold typography** — Dramatic size contrast (hero "Sky" at 14rem) creates impact; text is the primary design element
2. **Organic decoration** — Squiggles, blobs, and asterisks add hand-crafted personality throughout
3. **Editorial asymmetry** — Alternating left-right content blocks, numbered steps with dividers, not uniform card grids
4. **Marquee energy** — Continuous scrolling text strips add movement and community energy
5. **Olive warmth** — Olive-toned greens feel more natural and organic than saturated forest green
